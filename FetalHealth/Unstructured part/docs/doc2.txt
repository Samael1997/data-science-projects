Prenatal testing is a way to check to see if your  baby might carry certain genetic abnormalities, such as chromosomal or neural tube defects. However, chances are you're fine. Only 3 to 5 percent of all pregnancies have a birth defect or genetic condition. Many ob-gyns recommend testing if you fall into a high-risk category. Those with a history of medical abnormalities in a previous pregnancy, and those who are carriers for specific genetic conditions.
